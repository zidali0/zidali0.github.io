Welcome!

I’m currently a PhD student specializing in mechanical and biomedical engineering at the University of Michigan. My research interest is in micro/nanotechnology and its biomedical applications.

In my current research, I leverage the techniques in microfabrication to develop miniaturized devices for blood clotting testing as well as for fundamental molecular and cellular investigation. It involves both experiment and simulation, both solo grinding and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Outside of academia, I do a lot of physical exercise. I like basketball, badminton, running, cycling, etc. I once accomplished a half-marathon and accomplished a cycling trip across Hainan Island (about 200 miles). I’m living in a student co-op with more than 100 housemates from all over the world.

My plan after doctorate is to make the most of my intelligence and expertise and find a career in--but not limited to--healthcare industry.
