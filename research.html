Our research is focused on addressing problems in biomedical research using micro-/nano-technology. We are particularly interested in

  • Single cell analysis based on droplet microfluidics
  • Microfluidic chips for in vitro diagnostics
  • Mechanobiology

Some of the past and ongoing projects are shown below.

Single cell analysis based on droplet microfluidics

Bulk analysis takes ensemble average of the biological samples, while single cell analysis offers the opportunity to tease out cell heterogeneity. A typical pipeline of single cell analysis including cell isolation, library construction, sequencing, and stats, and droplet microfluidics offers a method to isolate and process cells with ultrahigh throughput. We are currently working on improving the performance of droplet microfluidics in single cell analysis, in terms of cell encapsulation, droplet sorting, and barcoding. We work closely with our collaborators in sequencing industry.

Microfluidic chips for in vitro diagnostics

Microfluidic chip is like a test strip that can be used for cheap and fast point-of-care in vitro diagnostics. Leveraging our expertise in microfabrication and fluid mechanics, we aim to develop microfluidic chips with real-life impacts. We are currently designing projects in this direction.

Mechanobiology

In addition to chemical signals such as growth factors, mechanical cues also play a significant role in dictating cell and tissue behaviors. Such mechanical cues include extracellular matrix stiffness, spatial confinement, shear force, etc. Currently, we are working on two projects regarding tissue-level and cell-level mechanobiology, respectively.

Blood clot is involved in both hemostasis and thrombosis. The formation as well as the function of the clot is affected by the blood flow and the contractility of the clot itself. In this context, one of our ongoing projects aims to develop a tool to measure the clot contractility in a hemodynamic setting. This tool, if successfully developed, would serve as a powerful platform for the study of thrombus formation and potentially lead to new discoveries on thrombosis prevention.

Micropost array (see the figure above) is an array of soft micropillars patterned on a substrate for cell culture and contractility measurement. We implement this tool in our lab and study the mechanobiology of different cells.
